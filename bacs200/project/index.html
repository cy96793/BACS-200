Air Jordan 1984

Air Jordan is a brand of basketball sneakers, athletic, casual, and style clothing produced by Nike. It was created after 6x NBA champion and finals MVP, 5x NBA Most Valuable Player, 14x NBA All-Star, Michael Jordan. The original Air Jordan sneakers were produced exclusively for Michael Jordan in early 1984 and released to the public in late 1984. The shoes were designed for Nike by Peter Moore, Tinker Hatfield, and Bruce Kilgore.

The most iconic Air Jordan sneaker is the Air Jordan 1 which was created and designed for Michael Jordan in 1984. The Air Jordan 1 made it depute to the public for only $65 dollars. As of 2020 the Air Jordan line reached number 34 and is being well represented by young NBA players like Zion Williamson and Jason Tatum. The typical Air Jordan retail price today is around $200 dollars.

Nike 1964.

Nike, Inc., formerly (1964–78) Blue Ribbon Sports, American sportswear company headquartered in Beaverton, Oregon. It was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Bill Bowerman a track-and-field coach at the University of Oregon, and his former student Phil Knight. They opened their first retail outlet in 1966 and launched the Nike brand shoe in 1972. The company was renamed Nike, Inc., in 1978 and went public two years later. By the early 21st century, Nike had retail outlets and distributors in more than 170 countries, and its logo—a curved check mark called the “swoosh”—was recognized throughout the world.

In 1970, Bowerman was experimenting by pouring rubber into his wife’s waffle iron and he created the waffle sole. Since then the waffle sole is an innovation that changed running shoes forever. Also, in 1972, Nike released the Cortez during the Mexico Olympics. The Cortez was a culmination of Nike’s track shoe development and the first to feature foam for cushioning. The Cortez is one of the most iconic sneakers for Nike and has been released in many different colorways throughout the years. One of the most popular color ways is the “Forrest Gump” made for the movie. Other really iconic and popular silhouette today are the Blazers and any Air Max sneakers.

Personally, my favorite Nike sneakers are the Air Max 97 and Air Max 90. I just love the style they bring and how comfortable they are at the same time. It is crazy to me that the Air Max 90 came out 30 years ago and it still very popular lifestyle sneaker!

Puma 1948.

To be exact, Puma was established in 1948, and the first shoe to release was the Atom a soccer shoe. In 1952 runner Josef Barthel wore Puma while he won a Olympic Gold Medal in the 1500 m. In 1956, the formstripe was introduced and since then, has been a Puma trademark. It wasn’t till 1968 when Puma brought the now famous “Puma” aka Cat logo to the sneakers.

1968 was a very controversial year for the African American communities and the Olympics. Tommie Smith, who won the Olympic Gold 200 m in Mexico was wearing a pair of Puma running shoes. He then took stand barefoot with the Puma’s by his side. At this time, teammate John Carlos and Tommie Smith made the Black Power salute, because of the mistreatment of African Americans at the moment. Mr. Smith then left his pair of Puma’s for the world to see. Later, both Olympians were banned from the Olympics.

In 1968 the Puma Clyde released, which is a basketball shoe designed by Walter “Clyde” Frazier, which today is one of the more popular Puma’s. Other professional athletes to wear Puma are Diego Maradona, a professional soccer player and tennis superstar Boris Becker, which he had his own signature sneaker the “Puma Becker” and “Puma Becker Ace”, releasing in the 1980s.

Reebok 1958

Reebok was founded in England, in 1958, by a pair of brothers, Joe and Jeff Foster, to carry on a family tradition of making athletic footwear. Their grandfather, Joseph William Foster, had launched an eponymous company, J.W. Foster, in 1895 and developed one of the very first track spikes.

The family wanted to call the brand “Mercury” but due to copy right issues they could not. Therefore, the brothers turned to the Grey Rhebok, a species of African Antelope, which inspired the brand name. Reebok continued to produce running spikes throughout the ’60s and ’70s, with the company’s base remaining in England. In 1979, Reebok was exhibiting at the Chicago International Sneaker Trade Show, when Paul Fireman, an American outdoors gear wholesaler, discovered the brand; Fireman acquired the exclusive rights to Reebok in North America.

Throughout the years Reebok worked with a lot of professional athletes and entertainers such as Jay Z, Alicia Keys, Tyga, Swiss Beats, Shaq, Shawn Kemp, Floyd Mayweather and probably the most popular athlete with Reebok, Allen Iverson. Allen Iverson had many signature sneakers throughout his NBA career. During his rookie campaign he wore the Reebok Questions which is a very iconic sneaker today. After that, he wore the Reebok Answer. Allen Iverson had a new model for every season, so the Answer’s go from 1-12.

The famous Allen Iverson crossover of Michael Jordan will never be forgotten. The sneaker Iverson was wearing was the Reebok Questions “Blue Toe”. That is part of the reason why I believe the Questions are one of the most popular Reebok basketball sneakers!

Vans 1966.

Brothers Paul Van Doren and Jim Van Doren along with partners Gordon Lee and Serge Delia open for business at 704 E. Broadway in Anaheim, Calif. on March 16, 1966. The Van Doren Rubber Company is unique in that it manufactures shoes on premises and sells them directly to the public. On that day, 12 people ordered sneakers and were ready to be picked up later that afternoon.

In 1976, the Vans #95, now known as the Era, with a padded collar and different color combinations, is designed by Tony Alva and Stacy Peralta and becomes the shoe of choice for a generation of skateboarders. In 1977, the Vans #36, the Old Skool, debuts with the now famous Vans Sidestripe and the the Vans #98 is also introduced and with the help of skateboarders and BMX riders, Vans Classic Slip-Ons become the rage in Southern California and will come to be the original Slip-On silhouette that will be known as an icon for generations.

By the end of the 1970’s Vans has 70 stores in California that shipped sneakers around the world. They continued to create classic silhouette like the Sk8-HI. Vans created the culture for skateboarding sneakers and offered them at a reasonable price so everyone could afford them. Today, you can see vans at any sneaker store in the mall and could buy a pair for around $60 dollars.
